The Easter Egg Event

Scenario

You are a web developer and you were contracted by a local business, Doug's Dogs, to build a website.

After building the website, the business informed you that they cannot pay you the agreed rate and claimed to be dissatisfied with aspects of the site they specifically requested.

You have them in a 12-month contract for hosting. Even though you are only making 10 dollars a month from the hosting, you can't afford to just sever the contract.

You decide to get even in a way only a developer can: You decide to plant an easter egg on their site.

Requirments

Hide a JavaScript event based easter egg in a local copy of this website

Put the lab in an easy to identify directory on the web server

Some Rules

Easter eggs are typically meant to be playful or silly references. Your easter egg should not feature explicit content.

Easter eggs as a phenomena in media are typically traced back to Warren Robinette, the creator of Atari's Adventure.

I once went to a conference where he gave the keynote

He spoke about fitting the code for Adventure into a miniscule space ~1440 bytes iirc, his work as a VR reasearcher at UNC Chapel Hill, and climate change

It was an awesome talk and my buddy and I approached him afterwards

My buddy says, "Hey, I loved your talk"

Warren gets really serious, stares daggers into him and asks "What did you like about it"

My buddy tried to save it by saying "It was really good, all of it"

Warren, never breaking eye contact, asks him "What specifically did you like about it?"

Right around that time, Warren gets pulled away somewhere else, while my friend stood there confused and I shook with joy knowing I would spend the rest of my life telling that story every chance I got.

But that is a digression, essentially in the early days of games, game developers were seen only as engineers, not artists

Because of this, the companies that published games saw no need to credit the incredibly talented and creative people actually making the games

In a bit of defiance, Warren hid his name in the art of the game in a place that was only accessible through a complicated set of tasks

Creative people have since used easter eggs as a way to get one over on the money people that are blind to them
